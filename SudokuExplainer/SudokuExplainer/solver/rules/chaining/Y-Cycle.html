XY-Cycle

The cells {0} form a XY-Cycle: there are exactly two ways of placing the values in these cells, forming two different possible configurations. Some values appear in a row, column or block regardless of the chosen configuration. Because one of the two configurations must be correct, other occurances of these values can be removed from these rows, columns or blocks.

The cycle is an XY-Cycle because only cells with two potential values are involved. XY-Cycles are also known as Forcing Loops or Bivalue cycles.

The two configurations of the XY-Cycle are enforced by two complementary, circular chains of implications. The details of each chain are given below.
